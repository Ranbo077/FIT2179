Australia's Changing Cultural Landscape

Australia has become increasingly culturally diverse over time. This visualisation explores how migration, birthplace, state-level differences and language use show the changing profile of modern Australia.

Where are overseas-born Australians concentrated?

The map shows the share of overseas-born population across Australian states and territories. Western Australia, Victoria and New South Wales show relatively high levels of overseas-born residents.

Source: ABS Census 2021, Cultural Diversity data.

How has migration changed over time?

Net overseas migration fell sharply during the COVID-19 border closure period, then rebounded strongly as international movement resumed.

Source: ABS Overseas Migration, 2004–05 to 2024–25.

Where do overseas-born Australians come from?

Australia's overseas-born population comes from a wide range of countries. In 2025, India, England and China were the three largest birthplaces among overseas-born residents.

Source: ABS Australia's Population by Country of Birth, 2025.

Which migrant communities have grown over time?

Comparing 1996 and 2025 shows that Australia's overseas-born population has not only grown, but also changed in composition. India and China grew strongly, while England remained one of the largest birthplaces.

Has Australia become more overseas-born over time?

Migration does not only change annual movement flows. Over the longer term, it has also changed the structure of Australia's population, with a growing share of residents born overseas.

Do all states have the same birthplace profile?

The national pattern hides important state-level differences. India and China are strongly represented in New South Wales and Victoria, while New Zealand is more visible in Queensland and Western Australia.

Does cultural diversity appear in everyday language use?

Language use at home provides another view of cultural diversity. The Northern Territory, Victoria and New South Wales have the highest shares of residents speaking a language other than English at home.
